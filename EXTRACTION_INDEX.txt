UNIT TYPES (6):
✓ Infantry - Core troops, high Toughness, adjacent attacks only
✓ Artillery - Ranged, can attack any unit, center rank
✓ Cavalry - Mobile, high Power, attack cavalry/exposed units
✓ Aerial - Rare flying units, attack any unit, few retaliate
✓ Levies - Untrained, auto-disband after battle
✓ Siege Engines - Damage fortifications, require full round between attacks

✓ ATK - Attack/Tactical effectiveness
✓ DEF - Defense/Evasion (opposed DC)
✓ POW - Power/Physical prowess
✓ TOU - Toughness/Hardiness

STATISTICS EXPLANATION
================================================================================

Attack (ATK):
- Measure of unit's tactical effectiveness
- Ability to successfully execute attack orders and engage enemies
- Improved by experience (up to +3 for Super-Elite)
- Used as d20 roll bonus in Attack tests

Defense (DEF):
- Ability to maneuver and avoid/minimize attacks
- Used as DC for opposed Attack tests
- Improved by experience (up to +6 for Super-Elite)
- Higher Defense = harder to hit

Power (POW):
- Physical prowess in battle
- Second part of attack (Power test after successful Attack test)
- Inflicts additional casualties on success
- Improved by equipment (light: 0, super-heavy: +6)
- Opposed by Toughness in Power tests

Toughness (TOU):
- Physical hardiness and ability to withstand attacks
- Opposes Power tests to reduce incoming damage
- Improved by equipment (light: 0, super-heavy: +6)
- Higher Toughness = more resilient

Morale (MOR):
- Ability to maintain discipline against overwhelming odds
- Used in Morale tests when unit becomes diminished
- Improved by experience (up to +6 for Super-Elite)
- Critical for unit survival in prolonged battles

Command (COM):
- Ability to correctly interpret complex orders and execute them
- Used in maneuver Command tests
- Improved by experience (ranges from -1 for Levies to +3 for Super-Elite)
- Essential for executing tactical maneuvers

SIZE & CASUALTY TRACKING:
- Size determines starting casualty die (d4-d12)
- Unit can take casualties equal to casualty die number
- When casualty die reaches 0, unit is broken
- Unit is "diminished" at 50% casualties or fewer
- Diminished unit must make Morale test or take 1 casualty
