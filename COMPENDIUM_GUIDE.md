## Key Statistics Explained

### The Six Unit Statistics

**ATK (Attack):** Tactical effectiveness and ability to land attacks
- Used as roll bonus in Attack tests against enemy Defense
- Range: 4-7 in Saxton's Army (Tier I: 4-5, Tier II: 5-6, Tier III: 6-7)

**DEF (Defense):** Ability to maneuver and evade attacks
- Serves as DC for enemy Attack tests against this unit
- Range: 8-12 in Saxton's Army (Tier I: 8-10, Tier II: 11, Tier III: 11-12)

**POW (Power):** Physical prowess and damage dealing capability
- Second part of attack after successful Attack test
- Opposed by target's Toughness (TOU)
- Range: 4-10 in Saxton's Army

**TOU (Toughness):** Physical hardiness and resilience
- Opposes incoming Power tests to reduce damage taken
- Range: 4-11 in Saxton's Army
- Note: Heavy equipment can add +4-6 to TOU

**MOR (Morale):** Discipline and ability to maintain cohesion
- Used in Morale tests when unit is diminished (50% casualties or fewer)
- Range: 6-10 in Saxton's Army
- Undead units (Grim Front) get bonus due to immunity to fear

**COM (Command):** Ability to interpret complex orders and maneuvers
- Used in Command tests to execute special maneuvers
- Range: 3-5 in Saxton's Army
- Officers and experienced units have higher COM

## Martial Advantages (Army-Wide Abilities)

All Saxton's Army units share three signature martial advantages:

**Skirmishers** (Infantry & Artillery)
- +2 to Attack tests if the unit moves before attacking in the same round
- Encourages tactical positioning and mobility

**Death Commandos** (Infantry units primarily)
- +1 casualty dealt on successful Power tests
- Makes Saxton's infantry particularly deadly in direct combat

**Patron's Curse** (Tier II+ and Heavy units)
- Causes disorientation on successful Power tests
- Adds psychological pressure and tactical advantage

## Using Units in Battle

### Typical Saxton's Army Deployment (Battle of Gravesford)

**Vanguard (Front Row):**
- 2-3 Human Infantry units
- Position to absorb initial enemy contact
- Supported by Goblin Sappers if fortifications are involved

**Center (Middle):**
- Human Artillery (Longbow) units
- Position in center or rear where they can target units across battlefield
- Provide sustained ranged firepower

**Reserve (Secondary):**
- Goblin Worg-riders
- Bloodfangs (if available)
- Ready to respond to breaches or exploitation opportunities

**Rear (Back Row):**
- Support units
- Commanders position here if directing multiple units
- Grim Front (if present and needed)

**Mobile Forces:**
- Cavalry units (Worg-riders, Bloodfangs) patrol flanks
- Ready to intercept enemy cavalry or exploit openings

### Tactical Notes

**Against Smaller Forces:**
- Infantry forms defensive line
- Cavalry flanks and disrupts enemy formations
- Artillery provides covering fire

**Against Fortifications:**
- Sappers to front to breach defenses
- Heavy units (Bugbears, Heavy Claw) to assault after breach
- Artillery to suppress enemy ranged units

**Against Cavalry:**
- Infantry holds center and sets defensive positions
- Worg-riders intercept enemy cavalry
- Bloodfangs provide heavy cavalry counter

**Against Aerial Threats:**
- Artillery focuses on aerial targets (if possible)
- Infantry groups tightly to minimize casualties
- Heavy units (Bugbear) to deal with grounded units

## Statistics & Balance Notes

### Unit Tier Progression
The units progress logically across tiers:
- **Tier I**: Basic trained troops, foundational forces
- **Tier II**: Specialized or enhanced versions, 1.5x power increase
- **Tier III**: Elite assault units, 1.5x power increase from Tier II

### Equipment Levels Used
- **Tier I:** Light equipment (Archers) or Medium equipment (Infantry, Cavalry)
- **Tier II:** Medium/Heavy equipment (Cavalry, Sappers)
- **Tier III:** Heavy or Superheavy equipment (Bugbears, Grim Front)

### Commander Bonuses
Each unit includes the commander's provided bonuses:
- Skirmishers: +2 to Attack when moving first
- Death Commandos: +1 casualty from Power tests
- Patron's Curse: Disorientation from successful Power tests
